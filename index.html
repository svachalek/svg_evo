Vector Painting Evolution

This page starts by creating a set of paintings that each contain one oval or triangle. These paintings are mutated by adding, removing, or altering components, and also cross-bred with other paintings.

The results are compared to the image labeled Target, paying particular attention to areas shown in the image labeled Weights. The dark areas in the Differences image show where the target image is most different from the best solution.

When a mutation is better than its parent or when a cross is better than one of its parents it will replace it. The process repeats as fast as possible, and after 2000 or 3000 generations you should begin to recognize the result.
